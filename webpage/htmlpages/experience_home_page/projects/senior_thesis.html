CrypticDB

For my senior thesis, I originally wanted to explore quantum cryptography. My original idea was to make a quantum toolkit that would evaluate quantum resistant cryptography and potentially simulate quantum attacks. I was in over my head . But at the same time, I was in a tough spot. My original idea which was going to basically be a cyber tool kit. It would have port scanning, malware analysis, and some other features I don't remember. Sadly this idea was shut down 2 months into my senior year because it wasn't feasible. Which is fair, too many hurdles and I'd need permission.


So my next best idea is to try out quantum encryption! After school, I'd sit in the comp sci building trying to learn about quantum mechanics and quantum computing. It was hard but I kind of understood the idea of it. I tried to make a prototype of my quantum toolkit but I needed a quantum computer. So I decided to use IBM's quantum computer, I used the qiskit library and barely made a functioning test program. I was able to get a qubit into super position. Trying to understand qiskit was hard, and the information I found online was outdated. Quantum computing is still new and I just don't have enough knowledge to learn everything in a short amount of time.


Still wanting to use encryption, I somehow thought about databases. I don't really remember how but I came up with an idea of making a personalized encrypted database. Basically, I would use a postgresql database and fernet encryption to locally encrypt the information inside the database. I would store the key using a python module called keyring. This wasn't a complex project looking back but at the time I was stressed. I had a lot on my plate at the time, I definitely think I could do a better job now.
